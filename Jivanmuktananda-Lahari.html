Jivanmuktananda Lahari

The Waves of Eternal Bliss

जीवन्मुक्तानन्द लहरी

1

Beholding forms of youths and maidens, richly adorned,
With golden ornaments, like painted figures drawn;
Yet dwelling with them, as a witness pure, self-aware,
The sage, anointed by the guru's sacred rite, feels no delusion.

2

In the forest, seeing trees bent low with fruit and leaves,
Shaded by dense foliage, echoing with birdsong sweet;
Reclining on earth, bathing in moonlight's gentle grace,
The sage, anointed by the guru's sacred rite, feels no delusion.

3

Now in a palace, now on a mountain peak,
By riverbanks, or in the hermit's humble hut;
Residing amidst sages, serene and wise,
The sage, anointed by the guru's sacred rite, feels no delusion.

4

Now playing with children, clapping hands in mirth,
Now delighting with young men and women decked in grace;
Now weeping with the aged, hearts burdened by care,
The sage, anointed by the guru's sacred rite, feels no delusion.

5

Now with scholars, deeply versed in sacred lore,
Now with poets, skilled in rhetoric's refined art;
Now with logicians, debating truths profound,
The sage, anointed by the guru's sacred rite, feels no delusion.

6

Now meditating, now offering worship pure—
With fragrant blooms, or spotless petals laid;
With heart uplifted, bowing in devotion true,
The sage, anointed by the guru's sacred rite, feels no delusion.

7

Now chanting names of Shiva, Vishnu, or the Chief of Ganas,
Whose blessings manifest in forms divine;
With tears of joy, eyes brimming with love's delight,
The sage, anointed by the guru's sacred rite, feels no delusion.

8

Now bathing in waters of the Ganga, or a well's cool stream,
In ponds or springs, hot or refreshingly chill;
With devotion pure, or with camphor's fragrant touch,
The sage, anointed by the guru's sacred rite, feels no delusion.

9

Now acting through senses in the waking world,
Now in dreams, still engaged with worldly things;
Now tasting bliss in dreamless sleep profound,
The sage, anointed by the guru's sacred rite, feels no delusion.

10

Now wearing hope's garb, or celestial robes,
Or bearing a deerskin girded at the waist;
Detached, a joy to hearts of noble souls,
The sage, anointed by the guru's sacred rite, feels no delusion.

11

Now in sattva's calm, now rajas' restless stir,
Now tamas' shadow—yet beyond the triad's chain;
Now a householder, now a wanderer in sacred lore,
The sage, anointed by the guru's sacred rite, feels no delusion.

12

Now silent, now engaged in speech divine,
Now laughing joyously, words forgotten, free;
Now observing the world's ways with tranquil gaze,
The sage, anointed by the guru's sacred rite, feels no delusion.

13

Now casting lotuses into Shakti's blooming faces,
Now receiving them directly from his own form;
Revealing the non-dual essence, beyond "self" and "other,"
The sage, anointed by the guru's sacred rite, feels no delusion.

14

Now dwelling with Shaivas, now with Shakta devotees,
With Vaishnavas, Sauras, or Ganesha's followers;
Beyond all divisions, established in unity,
The sage, anointed by the guru's sacred rite, feels no delusion.

15

Now beholding his own form as pure, formless, or manifold,
Endowed with diverse virtues, a wonder to behold;
Marveling, "What is this?" with joy unbounded,
The sage, anointed by the guru's sacred rite, feels no delusion.

16

Seeing all as truth non-dual, auspicious, divine,
Through steady practice of the great Upanishadic truths;
Beyond duality's illusion, chanting "Shiva, Shiva, Shiva,"
The sage, anointed by the guru's sacred rite, feels no delusion.

17

One who daily immerses in this liberating state,
The Supreme Shiva's abode, filled with grace and natural bliss—
Through guru's mercy, nectarine gaze diffused—
Is deemed a renunciate, yogi, sage, by seers thus.

Thus concludes the Jivanmuktananda Lahari,
A work of the illustrious Bhagavat Sri Shankaracharya,
Disciple of the revered Sri Govinda Bhagavatpada,
An ascetic-scholar of the Paramahamsa order.
